05 — How I Think

Two beliefs I've earned, not borrowed.

Trust is the wedge, not matching logic.

I've built scored matching systems and screened thousands of startups. The uncomfortable lesson: the algorithm is rarely the bottleneck. Deals between corporates and founders, or investors and founders, live or die on trust and expectation-setting. The best matchmaking work is mostly the unglamorous work of making sure both sides know exactly what they're getting.

Validate manually before you productize.

Every system I've built — screening pipelines, application engines, marketplaces — started as a manual process I ran myself until I understood it. Automation that skips this step just makes the wrong thing happen faster.
