01 The Thesis

The market prices specialists. It underprices translators.

Corporates, consultancies, and investors all sit around the same deals — and routinely misread each other. The corporate can't tell which startup will survive contact with procurement. The consultant models the market without having run the pipeline. The investor reads a deck without knowing what the founder cut at 2am to make the raise close.

I've done each of those jobs from the inside. That's the position: an operator who has already sat in your counterparty's chair — which is exactly what corporate development, advisory, and investment work all reduce to.

For corporates → strategy & innovation

I've run corporate venture programs from both ends — as the corporate (AB InBev) and as the agency serving them (Games24x7, Marico, L'Oréal). I know why pilots die and how to design programs that convert.

For consultancies → ecosystem & venture advisory

Screening 1,500+ startups builds pattern recognition no framework teaches. I bring primary, operator-grade knowledge of how innovation actually gets bought, funded, and killed — plus CXO-level client management under three-week deadlines.

For finance → venture, corp dev, allocators
